How to become a Kpop idol?



Though many companies will accept non-Korean trainees, you will still be catering to a largely Korean audience. It’s smart to learn about the culture if you aren’t already familiar. Study popular K-pop groups, spend time on Korean fashion websites, learn how to apply Korean-style makeup, and read up on etiquette and social norms.



Learn to speak Korean if it isn’t a language you’re familiar with. At a minimum, make sure you know basic phrases like “Hello,” “Goodbye,” “Please,” and “Thank you,” and read the Korean alphabet. You will have to sing in Korean at least some of the time if you become a trainee, and knowing the language will also help you make friends and get around in South Korea



Avoid scandals and be careful with what you put on social media. A big part of being a successful trainee is characterized by having good manners and behaving properly. Avoid situations where you might get in trouble, like being at a party where there’s underage drinking. Also use caution when posting on social media. Managers and executives can most likely see your accounts.
